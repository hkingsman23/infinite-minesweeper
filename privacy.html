Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-07-06

Infinite Minesweeper ("the game") is a free web game. This page explains what information the game uses and why, in plain terms.

What's stored on your device

Your progress — gems, the board you've uncovered, camera position, and your daily challenge streak — is saved only in your browser's local storage, on your own device. It is not sent to us or stored on any server we control. If you clear your browser data, or use a different browser or device, that progress won't carry over automatically (the game offers a manual "back up / restore" code for moving it yourself). We don't collect names, emails, or any account information, because there are no accounts.

Advertising

The game may show optional, opt-in rewarded video ads (you choose to watch one in exchange for in-game gems) served through Google AdSense / Google's H5 Games Ads. Google and its advertising partners may use cookies or device identifiers to serve and measure these ads, which can include using data to personalise ads depending on your settings. You can review and control this at Google's Ads Settings, and read more in How Google uses information from sites or apps that use our services.

Purchases

If you choose to buy gems with real money, that purchase is handled entirely by our payment processor, Lemon Squeezy, acting as merchant of record. We never see or store your card details — Lemon Squeezy collects and processes that information directly under its own privacy policy.

Children's privacy

The game is not directed at children under 13, and we don't knowingly collect information from anyone in that age group.

Changes

If this policy changes, we'll update the date at the top of this page.
